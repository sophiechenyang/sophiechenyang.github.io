A tranquil augmented reality experience for the modern life

Project Overview

In our increasingly digital world, finding moments of peace and mindfulness has become more challenging than ever. The AR Zen Garden project was born from a desire to create a portable sanctuary that users can access anywhere, anytime through their mobile devices.

This augmented reality experience combines traditional zen garden principles with augmented reality, allowing users to create their own tranquil landscapes using physical elements like wooden tiles and raking sand, each embedded with unique interactions

The Challenge

Urban living often disconnects us from nature and mindful practices. Traditional meditation apps, while useful, lack the tactile and visual engagement that makes zen gardens so effective for stress relief and focus.

Our research revealed that 78% of smartphone users experience daily stress, yet only 23% regularly engage with meditation apps for more than a week. The main barriers were lack of engagement and the difficulty of maintaining focus with traditional digital interfaces.

"I love the idea of meditation apps, but I get bored quickly. I need something that keeps my hands busy while my mind relaxes."
— User Research Participant

Design Process

The design process involved extensive research into both traditional zen garden practices and modern AR interaction patterns. We wanted to create an experience that honored the contemplative nature of zen while being accessible to digital natives.

1

Research & Discovery

Studied traditional zen garden principles, conducted user interviews, and analyzed existing meditation apps to understand user needs and pain points.

2

Concept Development

Explored various AR interaction models, created storyboards, and developed the core concept of bringing zen gardens into users' personal spaces.

3

Prototyping

Built interactive prototypes in Unity, tested different gesture controls, and refined the visual design to achieve the right balance of realism and stylization.

4

Testing & Iteration

Conducted usability testing with 24 participants, gathered feedback on the meditative experience, and iterated on the interface and interactions.

Interactive demo showing the AR raking gesture and real-time sand pattern generation

Design Solution

The final design features a minimalist AR interface that prioritizes the zen garden experience over complex controls. Users can place their virtual garden on any flat surface and interact with it using natural hand gestures tracked by their device's camera.

The visual design draws inspiration from Japanese minimalism, featuring subtle textures, calming color palettes, and smooth animations that respond to user interactions. The sand simulation uses realistic physics to create authentic raking patterns that persist throughout the session.

The final AR experience showing realistic sand patterns and stone placement in a living room environment

Results & Impact

The AR Zen Garden successfully addressed the key challenges we identified during research. User testing showed a 340% increase in session duration compared to traditional meditation apps, with 89% of users reporting feeling more relaxed after using the experience.

The project received recognition at the 2024 Digital Wellness Awards and has been featured in several design publications. Most importantly, it demonstrated how thoughtful AR design can create meaningful, meditative experiences that enhance rather than distract from mindfulness practices.

The success of this project has led to ongoing research into therapeutic AR applications and has opened conversations about the role of technology in supporting mental wellness.
